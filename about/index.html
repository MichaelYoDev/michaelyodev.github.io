why make this site?

For the past few years, I have been writing things down in my journal, which wasn’t always on me. I wanted to have a place where I could store my musings, explorations, and discoveries.

I have always been somewhat of an analog person, not really accompanying trends or being big on social media. When I discovered the concept of a digital garden, a website made not for sharing online but as a collection of notes and ideas for myself, I knew I had to join the movement and explore the roots of what the web was made for.

inspiration

The first ever blog I stumbled upon was the blog of Reece. His blog has writings on a variety of topics from the Lord of the Rings to the property of spin in subatomic particles. It opened my eyes to the realization that writing, even about random topics, can be interesting and even fun to read and write.

Recently, I discovered the website of Marius, as well as the Merveilles webring. There, I discovered the simplicity that personal sites and blogs can have while still containing lots of character.

how was this site made?

This website was made using Hugo and a heavily modified version of the Archie theme. I played with the idea of having a simple motherf*cking website with just html and css, but I found Hugo to be very fun to work with.
